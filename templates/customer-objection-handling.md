# Customer Objection Handling

Ethical responses to common sales objections. Adapt tone to your brand — stay honest, never argumentative.

---

## How to use this guide

1. **Acknowledge** — show you heard the concern  
2. **Clarify** — ask one question if the objection is vague  
3. **Respond** — address with facts, options, or boundaries  
4. **Confirm** — check if that helps them decide next step  

---

## "It's too expensive."

**Acknowledge:** Budget is a real constraint, and you want spend to make sense.

**Clarify:** "Is it the total amount, the timing of cash flow, or uncertainty about ROI?"

**Respond options:**

- Break scope into phases with clear deliverables per phase  
- Compare cost to status quo (hours, errors, missed revenue) if they shared numbers  
- Be willing to say no if budget and scope cannot align  

**Avoid:** Discounting without reducing scope; implying guaranteed returns.

---

## "We need to think about it."

**Acknowledge:** Thoughtful decisions beat rushed ones.

**Clarify:** "What specific questions are still open — timing, fit, internal approval?"

**Respond:**

- Offer a short written summary they can share internally  
- Propose a follow-up date instead of open-ended "call me"  
- Respect a genuine pause — close the loop politely if they go quiet  

---

## "We're already working with someone else."

**Acknowledge:** Switching has a cost; loyalty matters.

**Clarify:** "Are you fully set, or is there a gap they're not covering?"

**Respond:**

- Focus on one differentiated capability only if relevant  
- Suggest staying in touch for future phases  
- Never disparage competitors  

---

## "We're not ready yet."

**Acknowledge:** Timing drives most projects.

**Clarify:** "What would need to change for this to become a priority?"

**Respond:**

- Offer a lightweight resource (checklist, template from this repo)  
- Ask permission to check in at a specific future date  
- Add to nurture track — do not treat as rejection  

---

## "Can you guarantee results?"

**Acknowledge:** They want to reduce risk — reasonable.

**Respond:**

- Explain what you *can* commit to: process, milestones, communication, acceptance criteria  
- Share relevant experience types without naming confidential clients  
- Document assumptions in writing before work starts  

**Avoid:** Revenue guarantees, uptime promises beyond your control, or legal/compliance guarantees unless qualified to give them.

---

## "We want AI to replace our team."

**Acknowledge:** Efficiency goals are valid.

**Respond:**

- Reframe AI as **assistive**: drafts, routing, summaries — with human review  
- Point to [responsible-ai-for-sales.md](../docs/responsible-ai-for-sales.md)  
- Decline projects that require deceptive automation or non-consensual outreach  

---

## Internal note

Log objection type in CRM. Patterns over 90 days reveal product, pricing, or messaging gaps.
